Too Free to Be Free: Non-Ergodicity of Harmonic Entanglement on Random Gauged Hodge Laplacians

MU Javed. Manuscript.

Summary Overview

This theme studies random gauged Hodge structures, harmonic entanglement, and non-ergodic behavior in geometric systems with hidden constraints. The work asks how much structure remains visible when randomness is shaped by gauge transport, Hodge Laplacians, and harmonic subspaces.

The current focus is theoretical: understanding when apparent freedom in random geometric systems still produces persistent structure, rigidity, or obstruction to ergodic mixing.
