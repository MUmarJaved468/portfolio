Summary Overview

This theme studies how distributed agents coordinate through local interactions, directed graphs, stochastic triggering, and hybrid dynamics. The publications develop scalable synchronization and resetting mechanisms for pulse-coupled oscillators, rooted directed graphs, and networked systems where global behavior emerges from sparse local information.

The broader motivation is collective intelligence: how timing, graph structure, and local update rules shape stability, learning, and coordination in multi-agent systems.
